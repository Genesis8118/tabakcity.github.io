Tabak Festival

This enchanting legend reveals the intriguing origins of Tabaco's name, stemming from The Tabak Festival is an annual celebration in Tabaco City, Albay, marking its founding anniversary and honoring St. John the Baptist, the city's patron saint since Spanish times. Taking place on June 24th, the festival spans two weeks and includes vibrant events like and traditional dances, showcasing the rich cultural heritage of Tabaco. The festivities culminate on the feast day of St. John the Baptist, fostering community spirit and expressions of faith. In 2021, Proclamation No. 1164 declared the feast day a special non-working holiday, allowing residents and visitors to fully engage in the celebrations. The festival serves as a unique blend of history, religion, and community pride, creating a tapestry of unity among the people of Tabaco. Through colorful costumes, drumbeats, and prayer, the Tabak Festival has become a cherished tradition that goes beyond commemorating the city's founding, embodying the enduring legacy of Tabaco.

Padyak

Tabaco City has gained renown as the Padyak Capital of the Philippines due to its widespread use of padyaks, distinctive three-wheeled transportation. These unique vehicles are bicycles ingeniously modified with sidecars to accommodate passengers, becoming an integral part of short-distance travel within the city. The padyak has not only become a practical mode of transportation but also a symbol of Tabaco's local identity. Tourists and locals alike frequently embrace padyaks, appreciating their convenience and eco-friendly nature. Padyak drivers navigate through the city's streets, providing an essential and accessible means of transportation for residents. The proliferation of padyaks has not only enhanced mobility but has also contributed to the vibrant and dynamic atmosphere of Tabaco City. The padyak culture has become ingrained in the city's fabric, showcasing the inventive spirit of the community and its commitment to sustainable and efficient transportation. Through the padyak, Tabaco City exemplifies a unique blend of tradition and innovation, making it a distinctive destination in the Philippines.
