St. John the Baptist Chruch

Established in 1587 as a visita under the patronage of Saint John the Baptist by the Franciscans, Tabaco became an independent parish in 1664, separating from Cagsaua in 1616. Administered by the Franciscans until 1660, the church's supervision transitioned to the secular clergy from 1750. The current church, built by seculars, was finished in 1879, replacing an older edifice built in 1864. The church, designated as a National Cultural Treasure by the National Museum of the Philippines, is one of only two such sites in the Bicol Region, with its monument revealed on June 22, 2012. The church is constructed of black volcanic soil and local stones. The existence of mason's markings on stones used in the church's construction, an uncommon phenomenon in the Philippines, is one remarkable element of the church. Its odd floor layout includes chambers for which no explanation is now available. It is famous for its stunning bell tower with rococo embellishments.

Mayon Skyline

The Mayon Skyline, originally known as the Mayon Rest House, is one of Albay's most popular tourist attractions. It is perched 1000 meters above sea level on the slopes of the famed Mayon Volcano and provides a breathtaking panoramic view of the surrounding region. During the holy week, it is visited by both local and foreign visitors and changes into a hallowed site for the Alay Lakad pilgrimage. Because of its altitude elevation, it offers a unique perspective on the flawlessly symmetrical Mayon Volcano, making it a magnet for visitors seeking natural beauty and cultural importance. Mayon Skyline is a complex jewel in the center of Tabaco City, flawlessly blending stunning landscape with spiritual devotion.

KC Land

KC Land is a mesmerizing theme park located near Bognabong Chapel and the serene Memorial Park cemetery in Albay, Bicol. The park's perfectly groomed grounds and breathtaking views give a gorgeous environment ideal for picnics and leisurely outdoor activities. Visitors may become immersed in the thick foliage, creating lasting memories against a backdrop of bright blossoms and fascinating buildings. The environment of KC Land is a harmonic combination of entertainment and relaxation, providing a haven for people seeking escape from the hectic world. KC Land is an appealing retreat in Tabaco City, whether exploring the different activities or simply relaxing on the grassy stretches.

San Miguel Island

San Miguel Island, located in the Philippines' Albay province, is a mesmerizing location on the western side of the Lagonoy Gulf. This secret gem is governed by the city of Tabaco and consists of five beautiful barangays: Agnas, Hacienda, Rawis, Sagurong, and Visita. Agnas has beautiful beaches and verdant scenery that invite visitors to unwind in its golden dunes and blue seas. Rawis provides a taste of metropolitan life with bustling markets and colorful streets, whilst Sagurong is a paradise for adventure seekers, with hiking paths that provide panoramic vistas. Finally, Visita highlights the island's cultural diversity, with centuries-old churches and bustling festivals, transforming San Miguel Island into a diversified and enchanting vacation.

Hiraya Manawari

The Hiraya Manawari Nature Park, which is located in the picturesque Barangay San Vicente, spans an astonishing 3,000 to 5,000 square meters and incorporates an eco-friendly attitude into its design. The park, which is made of recycled materials, has seats and attractions that make creative use of abandoned goods, such as old toilets changed into seating and bottles repurposed into wall decorations. Visitors may enjoy the amenities, which include a resto-bar, playground, camping area, and picnic area, while immersing themselves in the natural surroundings. This creative and environmentally friendly solution not only adds to the park's charm, but also demonstrates Tabaco City's dedication to environmental protection and responsible tourism.

Adelaida Farm and Resort

Discover the peace and tranquillity of Adelaida Farm and Resort in San Vicente, Tabaco City, Philippines. Visitors may obtain entrance to a hideaway where nature takes center stage for a small entrance charge of 50 pesos. Two welcoming pools welcome, offering a refreshing rest in the middle of the tropical surroundings. The lush green lawns welcome picnics and long strolls, giving the ideal environment for relaxation. Adelaida Farm & Resort stands as an oasis of calm, giving a lovely respite from the hustle and bustle of daily life, with a commitment to a fresh and invigorating atmosphere.
