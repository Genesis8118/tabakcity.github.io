Origin of "Tabaco": A Tale of Love, Miscommunication, and Legacy

        This enchanting legend reveals the intriguing origins of Tabaco's name, stemming 
        from a humorous misunderstanding between Spanish conquistadores and a protective 
        father. As the story goes, the exclamation "Tabak ko! Tabak ko!"(My Bolo! My Bolo!) 
        was misinterpreted as a joyous welcome by the clueless Spaniards, ultimately naming 
        the city. Another version adds a romantic twist, describing a pre-Hispanic Tabaco 
        named Pagkamoot (Love), led by the warrior Datu Maisog (Brave). The tales, whether 
        funny or heroic, reflect the dual nature of Tabaquenos, embodying both warmth and 
        passion. Congressman Edcel C. Lagman emphasizes that Tabaco is the home of a proud
        and gallant race, where the name, like the iconic bolo, carries a balance of beauty 
                                            and strength.
